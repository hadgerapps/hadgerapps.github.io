Trivet

Her recipes, kept forever.

Photograph any recipe — a cookbook page, a handwritten index card, a screenshot — and Trivet turns it into a clean, typeset recipe card that lives on your phone. The stained card in your mother's handwriting becomes a card you can cook from, print, and pass on — and the original photo is always kept, one tap away, because the handwriting is the keepsake.

On your iPhone. Never uploaded.

Reading and typesetting happen on your iPhone with Apple's on-device frameworks. There is no account, no cloud, no analytics — capture even works in Airplane Mode. Your family's recipes stay in your family.

Cook from it

Cook mode shows one step at a time in large type and keeps the screen awake. Search by dish or ingredient, group cards into collections, scale a recipe or switch units with Plus, and print or share a beautifully typeset card.

Your recipes are never hostage

Your first 15 recipes are free, and everything about keeping them — viewing, editing, searching, printing, sharing, exporting your whole box as portable files — is free forever. Trivet Plus ($24.99/year with a 7-day free trial, or $49.99 once) adds unlimited recipes, scaling, unit conversion, grocery lists, and cook-mode timers.
