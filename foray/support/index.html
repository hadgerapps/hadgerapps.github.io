Common questions

How does Foray work?
Tap "Start a round" and Foray composes a handful of small, abstract quests — like "a door older than you." You go find them in your own surroundings and keep a photo of each. There is no map and no destination; Foray gives you a way of looking at where you already are.
Why does Foray ask for my location?
Only to confirm you actually went outside (that you moved from where you started), computed on your device. Foray never gives you a place to go and never shows a coordinate. If you decline, you can still play — photos just won't be outside-confirmed.
The quest library vs. Apple Intelligence — what's the difference?
On devices with Apple Intelligence enabled, Foray uses the on-device FoundationModels framework to vary quests further. On every other device, Foray uses its built-in quest library, which is the full experience. Either way, composition is 100% on your iPhone — nothing is sent to a cloud.
I couldn't reach a quest. Did I fail?
No. Tap "couldn't reach it" — there is no penalty and no streak to break. The round completes on what you did find.
How do I restore my purchase?
Open Settings inside Foray and tap "Restore Purchases." Because there is no account, restore is handled entirely by Apple via your Apple ID.
How do I cancel my subscription?
Go to Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions → Foray → Cancel on your iPhone. Cancellation is handled by Apple; Foray never asks you to cancel and respects it immediately.
Is my data private?
Yes. There is no account, no server, and no analytics. Your rounds, your location reads, and your photos stay on your iPhone. See the Privacy Policy.
A safety note
Foray is a game. Always mind your surroundings, respect private property, and don't enter unsafe areas. Skip any quest you can't safely reach.
