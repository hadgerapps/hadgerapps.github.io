The glance, not another planner

The Lock Screen widget shows the day as one readable band of light — night, blue hour, golden hour, day — with a marker for where you are in it. Before an evening that matters, start a quiet golden hour countdown as a Live Activity and watch it on the Lock Screen and Dynamic Island, hands free.

Computed, never guessed

Gnomon refuses to predict. There are no "sunset quality" scores and no forecast layers — only exact times from the same published solar and lunar mathematics used in almanacs (NOAA / Meeus). That is why it works in airplane mode, anywhere on Earth, and why it never disappoints you about a sky it couldn't have known.

Plan the light ahead

Gnomon Pro adds saved spots, scheduled Live Activities, and a month light-calendar that shows golden hour drifting through the season, with moon rise and set lines for photographers planning a full-moon evening.

Private by construction

  • No account, no login, no server. Your location is used on your device to compute sun times and never leaves it.
  • App Privacy label: Data Not Collected. The only network calls are App Store purchases and Apple's place search when you look up a new spot.
  • No ads, no streaks, no notification spam. The only alert is the Live Activity you asked for.

Questions

Does Gnomon need an account?
No. There is no sign-up and no login.
Does it work offline?
Yes. All light times are computed on the device. Only the App Store and place search need a connection.
How accurate are the times?
Sunrise and sunset match published almanac values to within about two minutes; golden and blue hour use documented solar-elevation definitions you can read inside the app.
Is this a weather app?
No. Gnomon shows what is exactly computable — sun and moon times. It never guesses about clouds.
